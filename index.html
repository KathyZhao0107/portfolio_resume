Course Case: Predictive Models For Movies Commercial And Reputation Success

Tools: Python

  • Brief Overview: This project aimed to predict the success of movies, considering both critical acclaim (measured by IMDb ratings) and financial success (measured by gross earnings). Using a dataset of over 3,700 movies from 1916 to 2016, we developed distinct models for predicting critical and financial outcomes. Critical success was classified into four categories — Bad, Fair, Good, and Excellent — based on IMDb scores, while financial success was evaluated through gross revenue predictions. Picked random forest classifier as the optimized model, archieved a 75% overall accuracy in predicting critical success categories. Key features included movie duration, budget, and social media engagement (Facebook likes of actors and directors). However, unbalanced data impacted the accuracy for the “Bad” category.
  • Impact: The Random Forest model for critical success highlighted the importance of movie duration and director popularity in achieving high IMDb scores. For financial success, models indicated that higher budgets generally correlated with higher gross earnings, though artistic elements such as genre played a critical role in critical acclaim.
